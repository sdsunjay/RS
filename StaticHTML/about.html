About Us

Diana Browning Wright, LEP, MS

Diana Browning Wright, LEP, MS, is an educational consultant who works with school systems across the country to improve practices and outcomes for students with social, emotional, and behavioral needs. She has over 40 years of experience in education, the founder of the PENT Network (pent.ca.gov) and has trained thousands of educators across the United States.

Clayton R. Cook, PhD

Clayton R. Cook, PhD, is a professor, researcher, and educational consultant with over 15 years of experience working with educators to adopt proven practices that address social, emotional, and behavioral barriers to academic success.

Lindsay Wright, MA

Lindsay Wright, MA, is a writer and teacher who has worked with students from middle school through university in the United States, Japan, and France. She served as Director of the University of Virginia Writing Center, trained new graduate student teachers on effective responses to student writing, and developed content for the UVa Writing Program online.
